## üî¨ About Me

I am a **Computational Biologist** with extensive wet-lab expertise, working for over 8 years in the characterization of novel proteins/enzymes and studying structure-function interconnections during evolution. I specialize in integrating wet-lab and computational methods.

Currently, I am focusing on **Protein Design**, developing custom workflows that integrate AI- and physics-based methods to generate binders for folded and unfolded epitopes of medically relevant biomarkers.

> *I want to drive innovation in protein and biocatalyst discovery, evolution, and engineering for industrial and biomedical applications.*

In my view, the future of biotech relies on self-improving AI approaches (Transfer Learning, RL) supervised on data to autonomously build models, hypotheses, and experiments. I aim to contribute to this future of supervised automation.

## üß¨ Current main Project: AI-Driven Design of CDKL5 Binders with Minimal Screening

<p align="center">
  <img src="Background/ensemble_animation.gif" width="700" alt="Protein Ensemble Animation" style="border-radius: 10px; box-shadow: 0 4px 8px 0 rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.2), 0 6px 20px 0 rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.19);">
  <br>
  <em><strong>Project Overview:</strong> Developing an AI-driven pipeline to target challenging, poorly immunogenic biomarkers like CDKL5. We integrate Machine Learning to design and re-design protein binders against folded and unfolded epitopes, optimizing for developability to distinguish isoforms. <strong>A key focus is achieving success with minimal experimental screening (<15 characterized designs), leveraging computational precision to bypass high-throughput bottlenecks.</strong></em>
